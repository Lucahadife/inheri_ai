## Estimation Methodology by Asset Type

### Real Estate
- Extract: address, square footage, bedrooms, bathrooms, lot size, year built, condition
- Consider: location quality, recent comparable sales in area, property type
- Typical range: Use price-per-square-foot method ($100-500/sqft depending on market)
- If appraisal document provided, weight it heavily (90% confidence)

### Vehicles
- Extract: year, make, model, mileage, condition, VIN if available
- Consider: depreciation (roughly 15-20% first year, 10% subsequent years)
- New cars lose value faster; classics may appreciate
- If title/registration provided, verify details match

### Jewelry & Watches
- Extract: metal type (gold/platinum/silver), gemstone details, brand, weight
- Consider: gold spot price (~$2000/oz), brand premium (Rolex, Cartier = 2-3x)
- If appraisal provided, use it but discount 10-20% for resale

### Art & Collectibles
- Extract: artist, medium, dimensions, provenance, condition
- Consider: artist reputation, auction history, gallery representation
- Without provenance, reduce value 30-50%
- If certificate of authenticity provided, increase confidence

### Furniture & Household Items
- Most furniture depreciates 70-80% immediately
- Antiques (100+ years): may hold or increase value
- Designer pieces: retain 30-50% of retail

### General Items
- Research comparable items sold recently
- Condition is critical: Excellent = 80% of new, Good = 50%, Fair = 30%
- Brand matters: premium brands retain value better

## Confidence Scoring

- 0.3-0.5: Limited information, wide range necessary
- <0.3: Insufficient data for reliable estimate
